Why BlueWater ROV?

A professional, ROV-first inspection company — not a dive shop and not a hobby drone.

Our mission is to deliver clear, actionable underwater information that helps you prevent damage, reduce liability and protect your investment. We operate under a simple philosophy: safety first, clarity and transparency, reliability, professionalism and innovation.

Commercial-Grade ROV Platform

We operate a commercial/industrial-grade ROV engineered specifically for professional underwater inspection and documentation. The system features a true 4K ultra-high-definition camera with live image optimisation, high-intensity adjustable LED lighting and an extended tether management system that maintains stable operation in low-visibility or debris-laden water.

Year-Round & Under-Ice Capability

Unlike traditional diver-based services, our ROV platform allows us to operate safely and effectively throughout all seasons — including the periods when underwater structures are most vulnerable. We conduct inspections after major storms, during freeze-thaw cycles and beneath solid ice using specialised winter and under-ice procedures for safe, controlled launches, navigation and retrievals.

4K Documentation & Clear Reports

Every inspection includes high-quality deliverables designed to give you clear, actionable insight into the condition of your underwater structures. You receive secure links to your 4K video footage, high-resolution annotated photos and a structured written findings report aligned with your preferred documentation format.

ROV vs. Diver Advantage

ROV-based inspections offer a safer, more efficient and better-documented alternative to traditional diver assessments for the majority of underwater evaluation needs. While divers remain essential for tasks that require physical manipulation or hands-on repair, ROV platforms outperform diver- based operations in approximately 90% of inspection scenarios.

  • Significantly enhanced safety: no human exposure to cold, dark or icy water.
  • Predictable flat-rate pricing vs. hourly diver charges typically ranging from $250–$450/hr.
  • Consistent 4K video and still photography instead of limited or no documentation.
  • Full under-ice operation where divers cannot access structures safely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from homeowners, marinas, HOAs, contractors and insurance professionals.

ROV inspections are ideal for most condition assessments where visual documentation is the primary goal. They are especially effective for docks, lifts, seawalls, marinas, swim areas, intakes and other structures where you need clear video, photos and a written summary rather than hands-on repair work.

Divers are still important when physical manipulation is required — for example, cutting, welding or installing hardware. In practice, an ROV inspection often provides the baseline information a contractor or engineer needs to decide whether a diver or other follow-on work is necessary.
Our core service area includes Allegan, Ottawa, Muskegon and Oceana Counties along the West Michigan lakeshore and nearby inland lakes. Most residential and small commercial projects fall within this footprint.

For larger marina, HOA or insurance assignments, we can travel beyond the core area by arrangement. Travel costs and minimum project sizes are discussed up front so there are no surprises.
Most residential inspections can be scheduled within a few days, depending on weather and lake conditions. We’ll confirm scope, pricing and any site access details before an appointment is booked.

For typical residential and real-estate projects, you can expect a same-day or next-day email summary, with full 4K video, photos and a written findings report delivered within 48–72 hours. Larger marina, HOA or multi-site assignments may take slightly longer; timelines are always confirmed in advance.
Prior to arrival, we ask that you ensure safe access to the shoreline, dock or launch point and that any gates or marina permissions are arranged. If possible, please clear loose gear from the dock surface and let us know about any known hazards such as shallow rock outcrops, cables or intake structures.

We’ll confirm power needs and parking when we schedule the visit. On-site, you’re welcome to observe the inspection and discuss specific concerns or areas you’d like us to focus on.
All underwater work is affected by water clarity, lighting and current. We use a commercial-grade ROV with adjustable lighting, but very dark, turbid or sediment-laden water can limit how far we can see at any given moment.

If conditions on the day of inspection do not allow us to obtain usable footage, we will advise you on next steps — which may include rescheduling, revising the scope, or recommending alternate approaches. We always aim to provide honest guidance rather than pushing ahead with poor-quality video.
You’ll receive secure download links to your 4K video, key still photos and a structured findings summary in PDF or similar format. These files are easy to forward to contractors, boards, buyers and insurance carriers, and you are free to retain them as part of your long-term records.

Why BlueWater ROV Services?

You’re not buying “cool underwater video.” You’re buying clarity, documentation, and decisions made with real evidence instead of guesswork. That’s what BlueWater is built around.

BlueWater ROV Services is a focused, owner-operated inspection service. The same person who answers your call also pilots the ROV, reviews the footage, and writes your report. That means consistent quality, aligned incentives, and one accountable name on everything you receive.

Commercial-Grade ROV Platform

We operate a professional ROV with a 4K camera, high-output lighting, stabilizing thrusters, and a long tether, tuned specifically for inland lakes and the West Michigan shoreline.

  • Stable, controllable footage in low-visibility water.
  • Ability to maneuver around tight dock frames, cribs, posts, and wall structures.
  • Live control on site so we can pause and re-run areas of concern on the spot.

Year-Round & Under-Ice Capability

The most useful information often comes in the shoulder seasons, when ice, storms, and fluctuating levels stress your structures the most. BlueWater is built to work those windows safely.

  • Under-ice SOP with controlled access points and vertical tether management.
  • No diver exposure to cold shock, entanglement, or low-light hazards.
  • Ideal after ice shove, major storms, or unusual lake-level swings.

Reports You Can Actually Use

Raw video alone doesn’t help you plan. We package each inspection so homeowners, boards, contractors, and adjusters can act quickly and stay on the same page.

  • Online folder with labeled 4K clips and key photos.
  • Plain-language summary with “Monitor / Plan / Act Now” callouts.
  • Documentation you can attach directly to estimates, bids, and claim files.

Local, Owner-Operated & Relationship-Driven

BlueWater is West Michigan–based and focused. We understand local lakes, bottom conditions, and the contractors you’re likely to work with — and we’re invested in long-term relationships, not one-off jobs.

  • Direct contact with the owner-operator from first call through final report.
  • Flexible scheduling for time-sensitive issues, closings, and insurance deadlines.
  • Transparent pricing with no surprise upcharges on the dock.

ROV vs. Diver: The Smart First Step

Divers still matter when repairs require hands-on work. But as a first look, ROV inspections usually provide more information with less risk and cost.

  • • Zero human exposure vs. diver risk in cold, dark, or turbulent water.
  • • Predictable flat-rate pricing instead of open-ended hourly diver billing.
  • • High-quality footage you can review multiple times and share easily.
  • • Cleaner handoff to engineers and contractors when it is time to repair.
